Achilles

Achilles is the best of the Greeks. Achilles is the son of Thetis and Peleus. After ten years of fighting, due to being disrespected by King Agamemnon, Achilles leaves the war. Achilles' fate and free-will are vital to the future of the Trojan War.

Briseis

Briseis is the prize and property of Achilles. After ten years of fighting in the Trojan War, Agamemnon's decision to take Briseis from Achilles leads to Achilles' refusal to fight anymore.

Agamemnon

Agamemnon is the King of the Mycenae and in charge of the Greeks. Agamemon's brother, Menelaus, entreats him to fight the Trojans when they steal his wife, Helen. Agamemnon's command of the Greeks after ten years could come crashing down when he takes Briseis from Achilles.

Andromache

Andromache is the wife of Hector. A princess of Troy, Andromache stands on the walls of the city with her son in her arms as she watches the fate of Troy play out before her.

Hector

Hector is a prince of Troy and the son of Priam. Famed for being the best fighter amongst the Trojans, the future of Troy rests in his hands as he tries to defend his city from the Greek invasion.

Helen

Helen is the wife of Menelaus and Queen of Sparta. Helen famous for her beauty is taken by Paris, a prince of Troy, back to Troy. This deception will cause a war that lasts for ten years and changes the history of Greece forever.
